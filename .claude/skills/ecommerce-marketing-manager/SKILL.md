# E-Commerce Marketing Manager — Cerebro General de Worker

This skill is the **central operating brain** for e-commerce marketing management. It synthesizes the complete Hub Central knowledge base into actionable expertise across all channels, operations, and decision frameworks.

## Role Definition

The E-Commerce Marketing Manager designs, executes, and optimizes the full digital marketing strategy for e-commerce businesses. This role sits at the intersection of **strategy, data, and execution** — understanding the complete funnel, delegating tactically, and making data-driven decisions.

### Core Objectives
- Grow online sales profitably (positive ROAS, controlled CAC)
- Retain and reactivate existing customer base
- Build brand presence in the right channels
- Coordinate team (trafficker, designer, copywriter) toward shared goals
- Report results clearly to leadership or clients

### KPIs the Manager Controls

| Category | Metrics |
|----------|---------|
| **Acquisition** | ROAS (by channel + consolidated), CAC, CPC, CPM, CTR |
| **Conversion** | Site CVR, AOV, Cart abandonment rate, Revenue per session |
| **Retention** | LTV, Repeat purchase rate, Email open/click rates, Subscriber churn |
| **Business** | Monthly revenue, Gross margin on digital sales, MoM and YoY growth |

## Cross-Channel Strategy Framework

### The Channel Mix for E-Commerce
- **Meta Ads**: Generates demand (interruption-based). 60-70% acquisition, 20-25% retargeting, 10-15% retention.
- **Google Ads**: Captures existing demand (search-based). Shopping/pMax for products, Search Brand for protection, Search Non-brand for high-intent.
- **Email Marketing**: Highest ROI channel. Flows (automated 24/7 revenue) + Campaigns (active communication). Can generate 20-30% of total revenue.
- **SEO**: Long-term asset. 3-12 month horizon. Free traffic once positioned.
- **WhatsApp**: Highest open rate (>90%). Use for transactional, cart recovery, selective promos. Most invasive if misused.

### Revenue Attribution & Deduplication
- **Ecommerce platform = source of truth** for revenue
- **Blended ROAS** = ecommerce_revenue / total_ads_spend (Meta + Google)
- Meta always over-attributes. Cross-reference with GA4 (normal gap: 10-20%, investigate if >30%)
- Email attribution window: typically 5 days post-click

## Monthly Operations Cycle

### Week -1 (Planning)
1. **Review closing month** (~2h): Revenue vs objective, ROAS by channel, email open rates, organic traffic, site CVR
2. **Define new month objective** (~1h): Specific revenue target, media budget, channel focus, key dates
3. **Plan campaigns & content** (~2-3h): Weekly calendar across all channels
4. **Brief the team** (~1h/person): Designer (pieces needed, formats, deadlines), Trafficker (campaign structure, budgets, objectives), Copywriter (blog articles, email copy, ad copy)

### Week 1 (Launch)
- Verify all planned campaigns are active
- Check Pixel/CAPI firing purchases, Google Ads conversion tag active
- Program first email of the month
- Verify email flows active (welcome, cart, post-purchase)
- Review Merchant Center for new product errors

### Weeks 2-3 (Optimization)
- **Meta**: Review ROAS by ad set, kill ads with CTR <0.5% at 1000+ impressions, scale winners +20%, check frequency (>4 = rotate)
- **Google**: Review Search Terms Report, add negative keywords, check Merchant Center
- **Email**: Review previous email results, program next send, check cart recovery flow
- **Team check-in**: Progress vs objective, blockers, adjustments

### Week 4 (Close & Report)
- Check budget cap, adjust if needed
- Extract data from all platforms
- Build monthly report
- Present to client/leadership
- Plan next month (return to Week -1)

### 4-Week Protocol for Major Events
- **4 weeks before**: Define offer, increase Meta acquisition budget +20% to build retargeting audiences, brief designer, segment email list for warmup
- **2 weeks before**: All creatives ready and approved, campaigns in draft, launch teaser/awareness, first warmup email
- **1 week before**: Verify Pixel/CAPI/tags, verify site handles extra traffic, verify stock, set event budget (3-5x normal), prepare war room assignments
- **Event day**: Launch at 00:00 or agreed time, email blast to main segment, monitor every 2-3h (ROAS, CPA, stock), scale +20% at midday if performing well, pause if site goes down
- **Post-event**: Lower budget to normal, "last call" email if extending, flash report, document learnings

## Andromeda & GEM Framework

### Andromeda (Meta's Ad Delivery System)
Andromeda uses LLMs to understand creative content and find the right audience. **The creative IS the targeting.**

Key principles:
- Broad targeting works as well or better than detailed audiences
- Invest time in creatives, not audience segmentation
- Pixel + CAPI together are mandatory (EMQ target: >7)
- Learning phase: 50 conversions in 7 days, don't touch anything during this period
- Budget changes >30% reset learning

### GEM Diagnostic Framework
```
G — Good Content Rate  → Is the creative relevant to who sees it?
E — Engagement Rate    → Does the creative generate action?
M — Meta Quality Score → Does the creative have good distribution quality?
```

**Diagnostic Tree:**
```
ROAS dropping
    │
    ▼
CTR > 1%?
├── YES → E is fine. Creative engages.
│   ├── Site CVR OK? → YES → Signal/attribution problem (M) → Check Pixel/CAPI/EMQ
│   └── Site CVR OK? → NO  → Landing/product problem (M) → Check UX, price, stock
│
└── NO → Creative doesn't engage
    ├── Hook Rate > 25%? → YES → Proposition problem (G) → Change copy/CTA/angle
    └── Hook Rate > 25%? → NO  → Scroll-stopping problem (E) → Change first 3 seconds
```

## Report Templates

### Monthly Report Structure
1. **Executive Summary** (1 page): Revenue vs objective, consolidated ROAS, CAC, top learning, next month focus
2. **Performance by Channel**: Meta Ads, Google Ads, Email, SEO — each with key metrics table + 1 paragraph analysis
3. **Analysis**: What worked (top 3), What didn't (top 3), Key learnings (max 3 concrete points)
4. **Next Month Plan**: Objectives, main campaigns, key dates, budget

### Weekly Report (Internal)
Quick table: Revenue, Orders, ROAS Meta, ROAS Google, Site traffic, Site CVR — this week vs last week + alerts + 3 actions for next week

### Flash Report (Events)
Event revenue vs previous event, orders, AOV, total investment, consolidated ROAS, best email, best creative, best product sold

## Alert Diagnosis Protocols

### ROAS Dropped >30% in 3 Days
1. Check Pixel is still recording purchases → Events Manager
2. Check site is working (speed, checkout, stock)
3. Check if frequency increased → creative fatigue, rotate ads
4. Check if CPM spiked → competition season, improve creative to maintain CTR
5. Check if CTR held but site CVR dropped → problem is site, not Meta

### Zero Conversions for 48+ Hours
1. Verify ads are active (not paused)
2. Verify budget available
3. Check Pixel: are Purchase events arriving?
4. Do a test purchase and verify it shows in Events Manager
5. If test doesn't register → Pixel problem, not campaign problem

### Ad Disapprovals
Common causes: prohibited words, special categories without declaration, images with >20% text, broken destination URLs. Fix the flagged element, request manual review, or create new ad if Meta keeps rejecting.

## Team Coordination

### Meeting Cadence
- **Monthly kick-off** (1h): Objectives, team briefing, deliverables calendar
- **Weekly check** (30min, Mon/Tue): Progress vs objective, blockers, adjustments
- **Monthly close** (1h): Final results, learnings, recognition

### Feedback Principles
- **To designer**: Always feedback on creative results (CTR, Hook Rate, ROAS), not just aesthetics
- **To trafficker**: Always cross-reference numbers first. Never give feedback on campaigns in learning phase
- **To copywriter**: Always feedback with data (open rate, CTOR, which subject line won A/B test)
